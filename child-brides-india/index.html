Daughters Not Brides: Child Marriage in India

Shreyas Mishra

Child marriage is a human rights violation that affects millions of children worldwide. It is a harmful practice that disproportionately affects younger girls. Although the prevalence rate of child marriage among girls in India stands at 23%, the absolute count conveys a horrifying reality.

Statewise Disparity

Child marriage is more prevalent in rural areas than in urban areas. The prevalence of child marriage is highest in the central and western parts of India.

Over half of the girls and women in India who married in childhood live in five states: Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, West Bengal, Maharashtra and Madhya Pradesh. Uttar Pradesh is home to the largest number.
